Chronological Resume

The standard resume format, listing jobs from most recent to oldest.

A chronological resume (or reverse-chronological resume) lists your work experience in order from your most recent job to your oldest. It's the most common format — about 95% of resumes should use this structure — because recruiters expect it, ATS parsers handle it cleanly, and it tells a clear career story.

In a chronological resume, your most recent and relevant experience appears at the top. The format assumes your career has been progressing, so the reader's eye naturally lands on your peak first. Each job includes a title, company, location, dates, and 3 to 6 bullet points of specific achievements. Older jobs (10+ years back) can be summarized into a short "Earlier Experience" line.

Example: A software engineer's resume might show: Senior Engineer @ Acme (2024–Present), Engineer @ Foo Inc (2021–2024), Junior Engineer @ Bar Co (2019–2021) — each with bullet points underneath.
