Soft Skills

Interpersonal qualities like communication and leadership. Hard to prove on paper.

Soft skills are interpersonal and behavioral qualities — things like communication, leadership, teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, and emotional intelligence. They contrast with "hard skills," which are specific technical abilities like coding languages or software tools.

Soft skills are genuinely important for most jobs, but they're hard to prove on a resume because every candidate claims to have them. Instead of listing soft skills in a dedicated section, demonstrate them through your achievement bullets. If you led a cross-functional team that shipped a product, that's leadership — shown, not stated.

Example: Don't write "strong communicator." Do write "Led weekly executive updates on a $3M product launch, keeping 4 cross-functional teams and 12 stakeholders aligned."
